Chris Says Browser Game

CHALLENGE

This application was completed for my Galvanize Q1 Project in which I was tasked with demonstrating my front-end skills. It had to be a fully-interactive site that used Javascript to take user input, store something in local memory, and return something. I decided I wanted to make a game my 4-year-old son could play, meaning it had to be something simple with minimal reading.

PROJECT

Chris Says Is a pattern matching game modeled after the old electronic game, Simon Says, from the 1980s. It has multiple difficulties and once selected runs a recursive function that flashes a sequence of red circles across the play field. Players must recreate the pattern which increases in length with each successful sequence until the either complete a full 16-circle pattern or make a mistake.

TECHNOLOGIES USED

Javascript ES6 * HTML5/CSS3 * Bootstrap * Node

Roo Lan Health Care Business Redevelopment

Challenge

As a family-owned company in operation since 1977. Roo Lan had relied on word of mouth advertising and b2b contacts for it's business for decades. However as new rehab centers and senior retirement communities began to pop up in the Olympia-area Roo Lan found itself outclassed and unequipped to compete for business direclty with customers. I was challenged as Roo Lan's first Marketing and Sales Director ever, to redvelop Roo lan's entire identitiy and business plan.

Project

As part of a comprehensive overhall of their entire business. I spearhead the transition of Roo Lan' outdated assisted-living building and rolled it into their existing memory-care building to create, "Roo Lan Memory Care". This rollout was accompanied by a new dedicated advertising campaign which included a brand new website, collateral, ROI tracking tools, and public relations calendar.

Results

During my 2 years at Roo Lan the following was accoomplished

  • Private pay census at the memory care community increased by 300%.
  • HMO/Medicare census at the rehab census increased on a monthly average by 40%.
  • Overal inquiry activity increased by 200% with a five fold jump in non-medicaid referrals.
  • Roo lan acquired new HMO contracts with multiple new providers in United Health Care, Soundpath, and Humana
